The Story

Built By One.
Played By All.

"I grew up in Baldwinsville, New York always thinking — why isn't there an app that just sends you outside?"

My name is Lucas. I'm an Aerospace Engineering student at Syracuse University, and I built SCAV. — entirely on my own, with zero prior coding experience.

The idea started in high school. I kept thinking about how people use their phones constantly but rarely for anything that gets them moving or exploring. I wanted an app that turned your real-world surroundings into a daily game — not a fitness tracker, not a social feed, just a straight-up scavenger hunt you race the world to complete.

When I got to Syracuse I finally decided to build it. The problem? I had never written a line of code. I taught myself everything from scratch — using Claude Code as my AI coding partner and leaning on the United AI Club at Syracuse University as a community to learn alongside. Every feature, every screen, every line of this app was figured out one problem at a time.

An aerospace engineer building a mobile app is exactly as chaotic as it sounds. But the same mindset applies — break the problem down, test your assumptions, iterate fast. SCAV. is the result of that process. Built differently, because it had to be.
